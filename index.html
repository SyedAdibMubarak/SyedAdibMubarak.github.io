Social Detective

My initial goal for this game is to create an artificial intelligence algorithms capable of generating random piece of dialogue to convey specific emotions, alas the process of creating such takes a large amount of resources and time that I could not afford, as such I utilized OpenAi API and used their Davinci Transformative Model to generate those dialogue. Based on the dialogue generated, I created a predictive algorithm to guess the emotion of the dialogue and output them as facial expression of NPC that I created myself to adjust the obviousness of their expression as to create a variety of difficulty. Player then makes their guess based on the dialogue and facial expression, but heres the twist, the predictive algorithm has a small percentage of error thus it will sometimes convey the wrong facial expression. It is the player job then to detect this discrepancy and if they do they will get extra points. The flaw of the algorithm allow me to create an extra feature that I would not have thought of, as one might say "It's not a bug, it's a feature".

Intergalactic Warfare

This project is akin to Space Defenders with all characters being spaceships and the only elements in the gameplay are player, enemies, and bullets. While it may look simple, a lot is put into its elements such as enemy movements, instantiating enemy waves, coroutines, bullet movements and object collisions. At this stage, I am much familiar with 2D Physics and it concepts such as the collision matrix. I discovered a new for loop type which is better in some cases, singleton pattern that are discouraged but have its uses, and also manipulating the particle system to produce the desired special effects. To further improve on the concept, I am thinking of taking inspiration from the hit game Vampire Surivors, adding upgrades that automate damage dealing and currency from killing enemies.

Outer Space Postal Service

My first elaborate Unity 3D project, I am more familiar with 3D movements though the Z axis is still restricted for the gameplay, I may make changes to that depending on the direction that this game will go. Played around with the rigid body elements and its constraints, scripting the movement based on collision giving me insight on when to toggle the natural physics. This is also the first game I incorporated with Unity's new source control repository, the process is fairly simple but will be limited in the future for bigger games and projects, so I might look into other alternatives. I also learn some refactoring tips that will help edit scripts much faster, 3D environment lighting, and automated movement with Sin and Tau. My idea to improve on this project is to add more narrative to it and also give player more tools to control their ship since I found it challenging to navigate simple obstacles.
